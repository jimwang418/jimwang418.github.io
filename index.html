Junxiang (Jim) Wang | 王俊翔

Masters Student, Johns Hopkins University

I am a Robotics M.S.E. student at Johns Hopkins University, currently applying for Ph.D. programs. I am a research assistant in JHU's Laboratory for Computational Sensing and Robotics (LCSR), where I am fortunate to work with Prof. Peter Kazanzides, Prof. Iulian Iordachita, and Prof. Chien-Ming Huang. I obtained my B.S. in Mechanical Engineering from JHU in 2023.

I am interested in designing robots that exhibit safe and intelligent behaviors that are human-like and/or natural to humans interacting with the robot. My research efforts at LCSR span the fields of medical robotics and human-robot interaction, with current efforts focusing on motion prediction, learning-based control, and large language models.

In my free time, I enjoy long-distance running (racing up to half-marathons), tennis, and photography.
